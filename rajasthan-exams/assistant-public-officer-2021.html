Assistant Public Relations Officer 2021

Introduction to Assistant Public Relations Officer

The Assistant Public Relations Officer (APRO) is a prestigious position in government departments and public sector undertakings. This role involves managing communication between the organization and the public, media relations, and maintaining the public image of the department.

Important: The Assistant PRO serves as the bridge between the government organization and the public/media. This position requires excellent communication skills, media handling capabilities, and a thorough understanding of public relations strategies.

Eligibility Criteria for Assistant PRO 2021

The eligibility criteria for Assistant Public Relations Officer recruitment ensure candidates possess the necessary qualifications and skills:

Educational Qualification

  • Bachelor's degree in Mass Communication/Journalism/Public Relations from recognized university
  • OR Bachelor's degree in any discipline with Diploma in Public Relations/Journalism
  • Some departments may require Master's degree in relevant field
  • Proficiency in English and Hindi languages

Age Limit

  • Minimum Age: 21 years
  • Maximum Age: 30 years
  • Age relaxation as per government norms:
    • SC/ST: 5 years
    • OBC: 3 years
    • PWD: 10 years
    • Ex-servicemen: As per rules

Experience Requirements

  • Fresh graduates can apply
  • Preference given to candidates with 1-2 years experience in PR/Media
  • Experience in writing, media relations, or corporate communications advantageous

Selection Process for Assistant PRO

The selection process for Assistant Public Relations Officer involves multiple stages to assess communication skills, knowledge, and personality:

Stage Description Marks
Written Examination Objective and descriptive questions on Public Relations, Communication, General Awareness, English Language 200
Interview/Personality Test Assessment of communication skills, personality, and suitability for PR role 50
Group Discussion Discussion on current topics to assess communication and group interaction skills 25
Document Verification Verification of educational certificates, experience certificates, and other documents -

Note: The written examination typically consists of two papers - Paper I (Objective) and Paper II (Descriptive). Some departments may include skill tests like drafting press releases or media handling scenarios.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Find answers to the most common questions about Assistant Public Relations Officer Recruitment 2021:

What is the eligibility criteria for Assistant Public Relations Officer 2021?

Candidates must have a Bachelor's degree in Mass Communication/Journalism/Public Relations from a recognized university. Some departments may accept graduates with diploma in Public Relations. Age limit is typically 21-30 years with relaxation for reserved categories.

What is the selection process for Assistant PRO?

The selection process typically involves a written examination followed by interview/personality test. The written exam tests knowledge of communication skills, public relations, general awareness, and language proficiency. Some departments may include group discussion or skill test.

What is the salary for Assistant Public Relations Officer?

The salary for Assistant PRO typically falls in Pay Level 7 or 8 as per 7th Pay Commission, ranging from â‚¹44,900 to â‚¹1,42,400. This includes basic pay plus allowances like DA, HRA, and special allowances. Exact salary varies by department and location.

What are the key responsibilities of Assistant PRO?

Key responsibilities include media relations, preparing press releases, organizing press conferences, handling public inquiries, managing social media, creating promotional materials, crisis communication, and maintaining positive public image of the organization.

Is there any specific experience required for Assistant PRO?

While fresh graduates can apply, preference is often given to candidates with 1-2 years experience in public relations, media, journalism, or corporate communications. Experience in writing, media handling, or event management is advantageous.

What skills are important for Assistant PRO position?

Important skills include excellent communication skills, writing ability, media relations, interpersonal skills, crisis management, digital media proficiency, event management, and knowledge of current affairs. Language proficiency in English and Hindi is essential.

Which departments recruit Assistant Public Relations Officers?

Various government departments recruit Assistant PROs including Ministries, PSUs, Railways, Defense, State Governments, and autonomous bodies. Major recruiters include UPSC, SSC, and state public service commissions.

What is the career growth path for Assistant PRO?

Assistant PRO can progress to Public Relations Officer, Senior PRO, Chief Public Relations Officer, and Director of Public Relations. With experience, they can move to higher administrative positions or specialized communication roles in government.
