Agriculture Supervisor Recruitment 2021

Introduction to Agriculture Supervisor

The Agriculture Supervisor 2021 recruitment offers excellent opportunities for agriculture graduates to work in government departments and contribute to agricultural development. Agriculture Supervisors play a crucial role in implementing agricultural policies and guiding farmers.

Important: Agriculture Supervisors serve as the bridge between farmers and agricultural departments. They implement government schemes, provide technical guidance, and help farmers adopt modern agricultural practices for better productivity.

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

  • Essential: Bachelor's degree in Agriculture (B.Sc Agriculture) from recognized university
  • OR: Diploma in Agriculture with minimum 2 years experience
  • Some states may require specific agricultural specializations
  • Knowledge of local state language
  • Basic computer knowledge preferred

Age Limit (as on 01-01-2021)

  • Minimum Age: 21 years
  • Maximum Age: 35 years
  • Age relaxation as per government norms:
    • SC/ST: 5 years
    • OBC: 3 years
    • PWD: 10 years
    • Ex-servicemen: As per rules

Selection Process

The selection process for Agriculture Supervisor involves the following stages:

Stage Description Marks/Weightage
Written Examination Objective type questions on Agriculture subjects, General Knowledge, Reasoning, Mathematics 100 marks
Document Verification Verification of educational certificates, age proof, caste certificate etc. Qualifying
Interview (if applicable) Personality test and assessment of agricultural knowledge 25 marks
Medical Examination General medical fitness check-up Qualifying

Note: The written examination is typically the main selection stage. Some states may have additional stages like skill test or group discussion. The exact selection process varies by state.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Find answers to the most common questions about Agriculture Supervisor Recruitment 2021:

What is the eligibility criteria for Agriculture Supervisor 2021?

Candidates must have a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture (B.Sc Agriculture) from recognized university. Some states accept Diploma in Agriculture with experience. Age limit is typically 21-35 years with relaxation for reserved categories.

What is the selection process for Agriculture Supervisor?

The selection process typically involves written examination followed by document verification and sometimes interview. The written exam tests knowledge of agriculture subjects, general knowledge, reasoning, and language skills.

What is the salary for Agriculture Supervisor?

The salary for Agriculture Supervisor typically falls in Pay Level 5 or 6 as per 7th Pay Commission, ranging from â‚¹29,200 to â‚¹92,300. This includes basic pay plus allowances like DA, HRA, and field allowance.

What are the key responsibilities of Agriculture Supervisor?

Key responsibilities include guiding farmers about modern agricultural practices, implementing government schemes, soil testing, crop management, pest control, distribution of seeds and fertilizers, and maintaining agricultural records.

Is there any physical test for Agriculture Supervisor?

Generally, there is no physical test for Agriculture Supervisor posts. The selection is based on written examination and academic qualifications. However, candidates should be medically fit for field work.

What type of agricultural knowledge is required?

Candidates should have knowledge of agronomy, horticulture, soil science, plant pathology, entomology, agricultural extension, agricultural economics, and current agricultural practices and schemes.

Which departments recruit Agriculture Supervisors?

Agriculture Supervisors are recruited by State Agriculture Departments, Krishi Vigyan Kendras, Agricultural Universities, and various agricultural development schemes and corporations.

What is the career growth path for Agriculture Supervisor?

Agriculture Supervisor can progress to Senior Agriculture Supervisor, Agriculture Officer, Block Agriculture Officer, and higher positions in the agriculture department. With higher qualifications, they can become Subject Matter Specialists.
