Agriculture Supervisor 2021

Agriculture Supervisor Recruitment 2021 – Apply Online

Rajasthan Subordinate and Ministerial Services Selection Board (RSMSSB) ने Agriculture Supervisor (Krishi Paryavekshak) पदों के लिए भर्ती 2021 का नोटिफिकेशन जारी किया था। इस भर्ती के लिए ऑनलाइन आवेदन 16 फरवरी 2021 से शुरू होकर 17 मार्च 2021 तक पहले हुए, और बाद में 8 जुलाई 2021 से 22 जुलाई 2021 तक आवेदन की अवधि पुनः खोली गई।

Important: Agriculture Supervisors serve as the bridge between farmers and agricultural departments. They implement government schemes, provide technical guidance, and help farmers adopt modern agricultural practices for better productivity.

Agriculture Supervisor 2021 : Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

  • Essential: Candidates must have a B.Sc. in Agriculture or B.Sc. (Hons.) Agriculture from a recognized university or institute.
  • OR: Passed Senior Secondary (10+2) Examination with Agriculture as one of the optional subjects.
  • Knowledge of working in Hindi written in Devanagari script is mandatory.
  • Good understanding of Rajasthan culture is also essential (for RSMSSB posts).

Note: No diploma or non-degree qualification was accepted for direct recruitment as per RSMSSB 2021 notification.

Age Limit (As on 01-01-2022)

  • Minimum Age: 18 years
  • Maximum Age: 40 years
  • Age relaxation applicable as per Rajasthan government norms:
    • SC/ST/OBC (Rajasthan): 5 years
    • OBC (Non-Creamy Layer): 3 years
    • Female Candidates (General): 5 years
    • Female (SC/ST/OBC): 10 years
    • Persons with Disabilities (PwD): Up to 10 years
    • Ex-servicemen: As per state government rules

Candidates are advised to check the official RSMSSB notification for the most accurate and state-specific eligibility guidelines.

Agriculture Supervisor selection process

The selection for Agriculture Supervisor (Krishi Paryavekshak) 2021 in Rajasthan is primarily based on a single written examination of 300 marks, followed by document verification.

Stage Description Marks / Nature
Written Examination Objective type questions covering Agriculture, General Knowledge, Mathematics, Reasoning, etc. 300 marks (Qualifying / Merit basis)
Document Verification (DV) Verification of educational certificates, age proof, caste / reservation proofs, identity proof, etc. Qualifying (No separate marks)
Medical Examination Check fitness and general health as per official norms. Qualifying / Pass / Fail basis

Note:

  • There was no interview / personality test stage mentioned in the RSMSSB 2021 notification for Agriculture Supervisor.
  • The written exam remains the main criterion for ranking candidates.
  • Only those candidates who clear the cut-off marks in written exam will qualify for document verification and final selection.
  • Medical fitness is essential before final appointment.

Allowances and Benefits

Agriculture Supervisors recruited under RSMSSB enjoy several financial and social security benefits in addition to their monthly salary. These allowances vary slightly based on state government norms and posting location (urban/rural).

  • Dearness Allowance (DA): Revised twice a year as per inflation index.
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA): Based on posting location (24%, 16%, or 8%).
  • Travel Allowance (TA): Covers daily travel and official duties.
  • Field Duty Allowance: Given for on-site field visits and inspections.
  • Medical Benefits: Reimbursement or access to state-run health schemes.
  • Provident Fund (PF): Monthly contribution to retirement fund under EPFO/NPS.
  • Gratuity: Lump sum benefit after a minimum service period (as per rule).
  • Pension: Post-retirement pension under National Pension Scheme (NPS).
  • Leave Travel Concession (LTC): Reimbursement for travel expenses during leaves.

Note: All benefits are governed by Rajasthan state government rules and may vary for Agriculture Supervisor posts in other states like Uttar Pradesh or Madhya Pradesh.

Agriculture Supervisor Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Find answers to the most common questions about Agriculture Supervisor Recruitment 2021:

What is the eligibility criteria for Agriculture Supervisor 2021?

Candidates must have a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture (B.Sc Agriculture) from recognized university. Some states accept Diploma in Agriculture with experience. Age limit is typically 21-35 years with relaxation for reserved categories.

What is the selection process for Agriculture Supervisor?

The selection process typically involves written examination followed by document verification and sometimes interview. The written exam tests knowledge of agriculture subjects, general knowledge, reasoning, and language skills.

What is the salary for Agriculture Supervisor?

The salary for Agriculture Supervisor typically falls in Pay Level 5 or 6 as per 7th Pay Commission, ranging from ₹29,200 to ₹92,300. This includes basic pay plus allowances like DA, HRA, and field allowance.

What are the key responsibilities of Agriculture Supervisor?

Key responsibilities include guiding farmers about modern agricultural practices, implementing government schemes, soil testing, crop management, pest control, distribution of seeds and fertilizers, and maintaining agricultural records.

Is there any physical test for Agriculture Supervisor?

Generally, there is no physical test for Agriculture Supervisor posts. The selection is based on written examination and academic qualifications. However, candidates should be medically fit for field work.

What type of agricultural knowledge is required?

Candidates should have knowledge of agronomy, horticulture, soil science, plant pathology, entomology, agricultural extension, agricultural economics, and current agricultural practices and schemes.

Which departments recruit Agriculture Supervisors?

Agriculture Supervisors are recruited by State Agriculture Departments, Krishi Vigyan Kendras, Agricultural Universities, and various agricultural development schemes and corporations.

What is the career growth path for Agriculture Supervisor?

Agriculture Supervisor can progress to Senior Agriculture Supervisor, Agriculture Officer, Block Agriculture Officer, and higher positions in the agriculture department. With higher qualifications, they can become Subject Matter Specialists.
